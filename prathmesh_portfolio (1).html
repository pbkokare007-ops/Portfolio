Capstone Project

Final year research — published at ICCMS 2025, IIT Bhubaneswar

  FINAL YEAR RESEARCH

Investigation to Enhanced Performance of Finned U-tube Shell and Tube Heat Exchanger Using Epoxy Based Polymer Composite Material

Guided by: Dr. Pramod Kothmire  |  Solver: ANSYS Fluent  |  Method: NTU Effectiveness & CFD

Objectives

  • Enhance heat transfer coefficient using epoxy polymer composite fins
  • Compare Nusselt number and pressure drop with standard metal fins
  • Evaluate thermal efficiency and structural behaviour of composite material
  • Propose composite material as a sustainable alternative for heat exchanger fins

Meshing Strategy

  • Hybrid meshing with tetrahedral elements
  • Inflation layers applied to capture boundary layers
  • Captures U-bend recirculation and fin boundary layers
  • k-ε turbulence model in ANSYS Fluent
  • Bell-Delaware method used for validation baseline

Key Results

  • Finned tubes: 15–25% higher Nusselt number vs plain tubes
  • Thermal efficiency gain: up to 22% with composite fins
  • CFD captures local turbulence effects missed by Bell-Delaware
  • Pressure drop increase within acceptable industrial limits
Published — ICCMS 2025, IIT Bhubaneswar

Conclusion

Epoxy polymer composite fins enhance heat transfer while significantly reducing thermal resistance. The finned U-tube shell-and-tube heat exchanger design was proven effective for compact industrial and HVAC applications. The research validates composite materials as a sustainable, performance-superior alternative to conventional metal fins, with potential for wide industrial adoption.
